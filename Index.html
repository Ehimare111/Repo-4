The Problem

You're not undisciplined. You're untrained.

Most people don't blow up their futures account because they're reckless — they blow it up because no one ever taught them the mechanics underneath the chart. These are the three patterns we see most.

// 01

Liquidated by leverage you didn't understand

You picked a leverage multiple because it "felt aggressive enough," not because you'd calculated your actual position size against your stop. One wick and you're out.

// 02

Paying funding fees you never see coming

You held a position through a funding window with no idea it was costing you, slowly bleeding a "winning" trade into a breakeven — or worse.

// 03

A strategy that's really just a feeling

You can explain why you entered a trade after it works. You can't explain it before. That's not a strategy — that's a story you tell yourself afterward.

The Approach

Risk management first. Entries second.

Every module is built in this order, because this is the order that keeps your account alive long enough to get good.

PHASE 1

Position sizing

Calculate size from your risk per trade, not your conviction. Every trade pre-sized before you place it.

PHASE 2

Market structure

Read trend, range, and liquidity zones without fifteen indicators fighting each other on your screen.

PHASE 3

Execution rules

A written checklist for entries, stops, and scale-outs — so the decision is made before you're emotional.

PHASE 4

Review loop

Weekly trade journal audits in the live sessions. Patterns in your losses get caught before they compound.
